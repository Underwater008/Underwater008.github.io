Homo Ludens is a term that means "playful human." It comes from a 1938 book by Johan Huizinga. The idea is that play is a crucial part of human culture and influences how we develop as a society. Play is not just for fun; it affects many areas of our lives like language, art, and religion. This concept has inspired researchers to study the role of play in various fields.

    Rules of play

  1. Play is free, is in fact freedom.
  2. Play is not "ordinary" or "real" life.
  3. Play is distinct from "ordinary" life both as to locality and duration.
  4. Play creates order, is order. Play demands order absolute and supreme.
  5. Play is connected with no material interest, and no profit can be gained from it
